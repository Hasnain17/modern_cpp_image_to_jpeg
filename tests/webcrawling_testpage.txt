<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Choosing_images">Choosing images</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Images&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Choosing images">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pertinence_and_encyclopedic_nature">Pertinence and encyclopedic nature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Images&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pertinence and encyclopedic nature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r911143475">.mw-parser-output .module-shortcutboxplain{float:right;border:1px solid #aaa;background:#fff;margin:0 0 0 1em;padding:0.3em 0.6em 0.2em 0.6em;text-align:center;font-size:85%;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .module-shortcutlist{display:inline-block;border-bottom:1px solid #aaa;margin-bottom:0.2em;font-weight:normal}.mw-parser-output .module-shortcutanchordiv{position:relative;top:-3em}.mw-parser-output li .module-shortcutanchordiv{float:right}</style><div class="module-shortcutanchordiv"><span id="MOS:IMAGERELEVANCE"></span><span id="MOS:PERTINENCE"></span><span id="MOS:IRELEV"></span></div><div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note"><div class="module-shortcutlist"><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcuts</a></div><ul><li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=MOS:IMAGERELEVANCE&amp;redirect=no">MOS:IMAGERELEVANCE</a></span></li><li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=MOS:PERTINENCE&amp;redirect=no">MOS:PERTINENCE</a></span></li><li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=MOS:IRELEV&amp;redirect=no">MOS:IRELEV</a></span></li></ul></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:APEC_Police_Helicopter,_Opera_House,_2_Sept_2007.JPG" class="image"><img alt="Top of an unrecognizable curvy building under blue sky with a helicopter so far in the distance that it resembles a sparrow" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/APEC_Police_Helicopter%2C_Opera_House%2C_2_Sept_2007.JPG/220px-APEC_Police_Helicopter%2C_Opera_House%2C_2_Sept_2007.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/APEC_Police_Helicopter%2C_Opera_House%2C_2_Sept_2007.JPG/330px-APEC_Police_Helicopter%2C_Opera_House%2C_2_Sept_2007.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/APEC_Police_Helicopter%2C_Opera_House%2C_2_Sept_2007.JPG/440px-APEC_Police_Helicopter%2C_Opera_House%2C_2_Sept_2007.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2048" data-file-height="1536" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:APEC_Police_Helicopter,_Opera_House,_2_Sept_2007.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>This image of a <a href="/wiki/Helicopter" title="Helicopter">helicopter</a> over <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Opera_House" title="Sydney Opera House">Sydney Opera House</a> shows neither adequately.</div></div></div>
<p>Images must be significant and relevant in the topic's context, not primarily decorative. They are often an important <a href="/wiki/Multimedia_learning" class="mw-redirect" title="Multimedia learning">illustrative aid</a> to understanding. When possible, find better images and improve captions instead of simply removing poor or inappropriate ones, especially on pages with few visuals. However, not every article needs images, and too many can be distracting.
</p><p>Images should <em>look like</em> what they are meant to illustrate, whether or not they are provably authentic. For example, a photograph of a <i lang="fr" title="French language text"><a href="/wiki/Trompe-l%27%C5%93il" title="Trompe-l&#39;œil">trompe-l'œil</a></i> painting of a cupcake may be an acceptable image for <a href="/wiki/Cupcake" title="Cupcake">Cupcake</a>, but a real cupcake that has been decorated to look like something else entirely is less appropriate. Similarly, an image of a generic-looking cell under a <a href="/wiki/Light_microscope" class="mw-redirect" title="Light microscope">light microscope</a> might be useful on multiple articles, as long as there are no <em>visible</em> differences between the cell in the image and the typical appearance of the cell being illustrated.
</p><p>Strive for variety. For example, in an article with numerous images of persons (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Running" title="Running">Running</a>), seek to depict a variety of ages, genders, and ethnicities. If an article on a general already shows its subject in uniform, then two more formal in-uniform portraits would add little interest or information, but a map of an important battle and an image of its aftermath would be more informative. Resist the temptation to overwhelm an article with images of marginal value simply because many images are available.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Image_quality">Image quality</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Images&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Image quality">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Use the best quality images available.  Poor-quality images—dark or blurry; showing the subject too small, hidden in clutter, or ambiguous; and so on—should not be used unless absolutely necessary. Think carefully about which images best illustrate the subject matter. For example:
</p>
<ul><li>An image of a <a href="/wiki/White-tailed_eagle" title="White-tailed eagle">white-tailed eagle</a> is useless if the bird appears as a speck in the sky.</li>
<li>A biography should lead with a portrait photograph of the subject alone, not with other people.</li>
<li>A suitable picture of a <a href="/wiki/Hammerhead_shark" title="Hammerhead shark">hammerhead shark</a> would show its distinctive hammer-like head, to distinguish it from other species of shark.</li>
<li>A map of <a href="/wiki/Moldova" title="Moldova">Moldova</a> should show its frontiers with <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>, so people may know where the country is located in relation to its neighbors.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rice" title="Rice">Rice</a> is best represented with an image of plain <a href="/wiki/Rice" title="Rice">rice</a>, not <a href="/wiki/Fried_rice" title="Fried rice">fried rice</a>.</li>
<li>Intangible concepts can be illustrated; for example, a cat with its claws out portrays <a href="/wiki/Aggression" title="Aggression">aggression</a>.</li></ul>
<p>Pages using seals, flags, banners, logos, or other symbols to represent governments, organizations, and institutions should use the version prescribed by that entity when available. These are preferable to amateur creations of similar quality, including photographs of physical representations of emblems.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Avoid_entering_textual_information_as_images">Avoid entering textual information as images</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Images&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Avoid entering textual information as images">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Do not use images to express textual information in place of real text. See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style#Avoid_entering_textual_information_as_images" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style">Avoid entering textual information as images</a> for more information.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Scale_references">Scale references</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Images&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Scale references">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r911143475"/><div class="module-shortcutanchordiv"><span id="WP:FIDUCIAL"></span></div><div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note"><div class="module-shortcutlist"><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a></div><ul><li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:FIDUCIAL&amp;redirect=no">WP:FIDUCIAL</a></span></li></ul></div>
<p>An image sometimes includes a familiar object to communicate scale. Such <i><a href="/wiki/Fiducial_marker" title="Fiducial marker">fiducial markers</a></i> should be as culturally universal and standardized as possible: <a href="/wiki/Ruler" title="Ruler">rulers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Match" title="Match">matches</a>, <a href="/wiki/Battery_(electricity)" class="mw-redirect" title="Battery (electricity)">batteries</a>, <a href="/wiki/Playing_card" title="Playing card">playing cards</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pen" title="Pen">pens</a>/<a href="/wiki/Pencil" title="Pencil">pencils</a>, <a href="/wiki/CD" class="mw-redirect" title="CD">CDs</a>/<a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVDs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Soda_can" class="mw-redirect" title="Soda can">soda cans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Football_(ball)" title="Football (ball)">footballs</a> (soccer balls), people, vehicles, and famous structures such as the <a href="/wiki/Eiffel_Tower" title="Eiffel Tower">Eiffel Tower</a> are good choices, but many others are possible. Such objects as coins, banknotes, and sheets of paper are less satisfactory because they are specific to given locales, but may be better than none at all since at least the general scale is still communicated.
</p><p>Quantitative data, if available, should still be given in the caption or the article.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Offensive_images">Offensive images</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Images&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Offensive images">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main page: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Offensive_material" title="Wikipedia:Offensive material">Wikipedia:Offensive material</a></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Image_blacklist" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Image blacklist">Wikipedia:Image blacklist</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:What_Wikipedia_is_not#Wikipedia_is_not_censored" title="Wikipedia:What Wikipedia is not">Wikipedia is not censored</a>: its mission is to present information, including information which some may find offensive. However, a potentially offensive image—one that would be considered vulgar or obscene by typical Wikipedia readers<sup id="cite_ref-typical_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-typical-1">&#91;nb 1&#93;</a></sup>—should be included only if it is treated in an encyclopedic manner i.e. only if its omission would cause the article to be less informative, relevant, or accurate, and no equally suitable alternative is available. Per <a href="https://foundation.wikimedia.org/wiki/Resolution:Controversial_content" class="extiw" title="wmf:Resolution:Controversial content">the Foundation</a>, controversial images should follow the "principle of least astonishment": images should respect conventional expectations of readers for a given topic as much as is possible without sacrificing the quality of the article. Avoid images that contain irrelevant or extraneous elements that might seem offensive or harassing to readers; for example, photographs taken in a pornographic context would normally be inappropriate for articles about <a href="/wiki/Human_anatomy" class="mw-redirect" title="Human anatomy">human anatomy</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Images_for_the_lead">Images for the lead</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Images&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Images for the lead">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r911143475"/><div class="module-shortcutanchordiv"><span id="MOS:LEADIMAGE"></span><span id="MOS:IMAGELEAD"></span></div><div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note"><div class="module-shortcutlist"><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcuts</a></div><ul><li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=MOS:LEADIMAGE&amp;redirect=no">MOS:LEADIMAGE</a></span></li><li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=MOS:IMAGELEAD&amp;redirect=no">MOS:IMAGELEAD</a></span></li></ul></div>
<p>It is common for an article's lead or infobox to carry a representative image—such as of a person or place, a book or album cover—to give readers visual confirmation that they've arrived at the right page.
</p><p>For some topics, selecting the lead image can be difficult. While Wikipedia is not censored, lead images should be selected with care <span role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable selfref">(see <a href="#Offensive_images">§&#160;Offensive images</a>, above)</span>. The lead image is perhaps the first thing to catch the reader's eye, so avoid lead images that readers would not expect to see there. Unlike other content beyond the lead, the lead image should be chosen with these considerations in mind.
</p><p>Advice on selecting a lead image includes:
</p>
<ul><li>Lead images should be natural and appropriate representations of the topic; they should not only illustrate the topic specifically, but also be the type of image used for similar purposes in high-quality reference works, and therefore what our readers will expect to see. Lead images are not required, and not having a lead image may be the best solution if there is no easy representation of the topic.</li></ul>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r911143475"/><div class="module-shortcutanchordiv"><span id="MOS:SHOCK"></span><span id="MOS:SHOCKVALUE"></span></div><div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note"><div class="module-shortcutlist"><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcuts</a></div><ul><li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=MOS:SHOCK&amp;redirect=no">MOS:SHOCK</a></span></li><li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=MOS:SHOCKVALUE&amp;redirect=no">MOS:SHOCKVALUE</a></span></li></ul></div>
<ul><li><span id="SHOCK"></span> Lead images should be of least shock value; an alternative image that accurately represents the topic without shock value should always be preferred. For example, using an image of deportees being subjected to selection as the lead image at <a href="/wiki/Special:PermanentLink/460749801" title="Special:PermanentLink/460749801">this version</a> of <a href="/wiki/Holocaust" class="mw-redirect" title="Holocaust">Holocaust</a> is far preferable to the appropriate images that appear later in the article that show the treatment of the prisoners or corpses from the camps.</li>
<li>Sometimes it is impossible to avoid using a lead image with perceived shock value, for example in articles on human genitalia. Editors may assume, per <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Content_disclaimer" title="Wikipedia:Content disclaimer">Wikipedia:Content disclaimer</a>, that readers are aware that such articles may contain such images.</li>
<li><span id="NOETHNICGALLERIES"></span><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r911143475"/><div class="module-shortcutanchordiv"><span id="MOS:NOETHNICGALLERIES"></span></div><div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note"><div class="module-shortcutlist"><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a></div><ul><li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=MOS:NOETHNICGALLERIES&amp;redirect=no">MOS:NOETHNICGALLERIES</a></span></li></ul></div>Articles about ethnic groups or similarly large human populations should not be illustrated by a photomontage or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Picture_tutorial#Galleries" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Picture tutorial">gallery of images</a> of group members; see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia_talk:Manual_of_Style/Images/Archive_6" title="Wikipedia talk:Manual of Style/Images/Archive 6">this RfC</a> for most recent consensus discussion on the topic.</li></ul>
<p>On some mobile platforms an article's first image may be displayed at the top of the article, even if it appears well into the article in the desktop view. When placing images consider whether this phenomenon may mislead or confuse readers using mobile devices.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_cycles_of_images">Life cycles of images</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Images&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Life cycles of images">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sometimes an image is removed from articles in favor of an image of higher quality or greater pertinence, or with a friendlier licensing status. However, such superseded images should not be deleted, so that Wikipedia's historical record is preserved; the history of articles becomes confused if disused images and templates are deleted. On the other hand, images removed because it is discovered that they violate copyright, privacy, or similar considerations should be listed at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Files_for_discussion" title="Wikipedia:Files for discussion">Wikipedia:Files for discussion</a> for potential deletion. Likewise, replaced fair use images no longer used in any article must be deleted.
</p><p>If you find an exceptionally good image, you are encouraged to nominate it as a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Featured_picture_candidate" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Featured picture candidate">Wikipedia:Featured picture candidate</a>.
</p>

<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r911143475"/><div class="module-shortcutanchordiv"><span id="MOS:IMGSYN"></span><span id="MOS:IMAGESYNTAX"></span></div><div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note"><div class="module-shortcutlist"><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcuts</a></div><ul><li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=MOS:IMGSYN&amp;redirect=no">MOS:IMGSYN</a></span></li><li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=MOS:IMAGESYNTAX&amp;redirect=no">MOS:IMAGESYNTAX</a></span></li></ul></div>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Syntax"><span id="Image_syntax"></span>Syntax</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Images&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Syntax">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Siberian_Husky_pho.jpg" class="image"><img alt="A white dog in a harness playfully nuzzles a young boy" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Siberian_Husky_pho.jpg/220px-Siberian_Husky_pho.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="209" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Siberian_Husky_pho.jpg/330px-Siberian_Husky_pho.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Siberian_Husky_pho.jpg/440px-Siberian_Husky_pho.jpg 2x" data-file-width="750" data-file-height="711" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Siberian_Husky_pho.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A <a href="/wiki/Siberian_Husky" title="Siberian Husky">Siberian Husky</a> used as a pack animal</div></div></div>
<p><i>Basic example (producing the image at right):</i>
</p>
<blockquote class="example example-block" style="font-family: Georgia, &#39;DejaVu Serif&#39;, serif; color: #006400;"><p><code>[[File:Siberian Husky pho.jpg|thumb|alt=A white dog in a harness playfully nuzzles a young boy |A [[Siberian Husky]] used as a pack animal]]</code></p></blockquote>
<ul><li><code>File:Siberian Husky pho.jpg</code> The file (image) name must be exact (including capitalization, punctuation and spacing) and must include <code>.jpg</code>, <code>.png</code> or other extension. (<code>Image:</code> and <code>File:</code> work the same.) If Wikipedia and Wikimedia Commons both have an image with the specified name, the Wikipedia version is the one that will appear in the article.</li>
<li><code>thumb</code> is required in most cases</li>
<li><code>alt=A white dog in a harness playfully nuzzles a young boy</code> <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Alternative_text_for_images" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Alternative text for images">Alt text</a> is meant for those who cannot see the image; unlike the caption, it summarizes the image's <em>appearance</em>.</li>
<li><code>A [[Siberian Husky]] used as a pack animal</code> The <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Captions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Captions">caption</a> comes last, and gives the meaning or significance of the image.</li></ul>
<p>See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Extended_image_syntax" title="Wikipedia:Extended image syntax">WP:Extended image syntax</a> for further features and options. If the image does not display after you have carefully checked the syntax, it may have been <a href="/wiki/MediaWiki:Bad_image_list" title="MediaWiki:Bad image list">blacklisted</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Size">Size</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Images&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Size">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r911143475"/><div class="module-shortcutanchordiv"><span id="MOS:IMGSIZE"></span><span id="MOS:UPRIGHT"></span></div><div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note"><div class="module-shortcutlist"><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcuts</a></div><ul><li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=MOS:IMGSIZE&amp;redirect=no">MOS:IMGSIZE</a></span></li><li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=MOS:UPRIGHT&amp;redirect=no">MOS:UPRIGHT</a></span></li></ul></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Image_use_policy#Displayed_image_size" title="Wikipedia:Image use policy">Wikipedia:Image use policy §&#160;Displayed image size</a></div>
<ul><li>An image's size is controlled by changing its width&#160;&#8211;&#32;after which software automatically adjusts height in proportion. (Most references to an image's "size" really mean its width.)</li>
<li><span id="Image_preferences"></span>Each user has a "base" width, which applies to <code>|thumb</code> and <code>|frameless</code> images; for <em>unregistered</em> users (the vast majority of readers) this is always 220 pixels; for <em>registered</em> (logged-in) users, the base width is 220px when the user's account is created, but can be changed via <a href="/wiki/Help:Preferences#Files" title="Help:Preferences">Preferences</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-leave_pref_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-leave_pref-2">&#91;nb 2&#93;</a></sup> The Siberian Husky image above is displayed at whatever <em>your</em> base width is.</li></ul>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:392px;"><a href="/wiki/File:William_Powell_Frith_-_The_Derby_Day_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/William_Powell_Frith_-_The_Derby_Day_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg/390px-William_Powell_Frith_-_The_Derby_Day_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg" decoding="async" width="390" height="172" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/William_Powell_Frith_-_The_Derby_Day_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg/585px-William_Powell_Frith_-_The_Derby_Day_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/William_Powell_Frith_-_The_Derby_Day_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg/780px-William_Powell_Frith_-_The_Derby_Day_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4001" data-file-height="1761" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:William_Powell_Frith_-_The_Derby_Day_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Image using width <code>upright=1.75</code>, so that it is 75% wider than the Siberian Husky image above, which is at the default <code>upright=1</code> width.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:112px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Phineas_gage_-_1868_skull_diagram.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Phineas_gage_-_1868_skull_diagram.jpg/110px-Phineas_gage_-_1868_skull_diagram.jpg" decoding="async" width="110" height="156" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/28/Phineas_gage_-_1868_skull_diagram.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="165" data-file-height="234" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Phineas_gage_-_1868_skull_diagram.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Image using <code>upright=0.5</code>; a scaling factor less than 1 contracts the image width.</div></div></div>
<ul><li>Where a smaller or larger image is appropriate, use <code class="nowrap">&#124;upright&#61;<var style="padding-right: 1px;">scaling factor</var></code>, which expands or contracts the image by a factor <em>relative to the user's base width</em>.
<ul><li>For example:
<ul><li><code>upright=1.3</code> might be used for an image with fine detail (e.g. a map or diagram) to render it "30% larger than this user generally wants". (For a reader with the usual base width setting of 220px, this is 285px.)</li>
<li><code>upright=0.6</code> might be used for an image with little detail (e.g. a simple drawing or flag) which can be adequately displayed "40% smaller than this user generally wants". (For a reader with the usual base width setting of 220px, this is 130px.)</li></ul></li>
<li>Short, wide images often call for <code>upright</code> of 1 or greater; tall, narrow images may look best with <code>upright</code> of 1 or less.</li>
<li>When specifying <code>upright=</code> values greater than 1, take care to balance the need to reveal detail against the danger of overwhelming surrounding article text.
<ul><li>Images in which a small region of detail is important (but cropping to that region is unacceptable) may need to be larger than normal, but <code>upright=1.8</code> should usually be the largest value for images floated beside text.</li>
<li>Lead images should usually use <code>upright=1.35</code> at most.</li></ul></li>
<li>Images within an article, especially those near one another and on the same side, may be more appealing if presented at the same width.</li>
<li><img alt="⚠" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/1/15/Ambox_warning_pn.svg/20px-Ambox_warning_pn.svg.png" decoding="async" width="20" height="17" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/1/15/Ambox_warning_pn.svg/30px-Ambox_warning_pn.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/1/15/Ambox_warning_pn.svg/40px-Ambox_warning_pn.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="445" />&#160;<b>Warning</b> If <code>upright</code> is completely absent, that's equivalent to <code>upright=1</code>. But <code>upright</code> <u>alone</u>, with no <span class="nowrap"><code>=<var style="padding-right: 1px;">scaling factor</var></code></span> (e.g. <span class="nowrap"><code>[[File:Dog.jpg|thumb|upright|A big dog]]</code>)</span> is equivalent to <code>upright=0.75</code>; this usage is confusing and therefore deprecated.</li></ul></li>
<li>Where absolutely necessary, a fixed width in pixels (e.g. <code>17px</code>) may be specified. This, however, ignores the user's base width setting, so <strong><span class="nowrap"><code>upright=<var style="padding-right: 1px;">scaling factor</var></code></span> is preferred whenever possible</strong>.<sup id="cite_ref-corresp_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-corresp-3">&#91;nb 3&#93;</a></sup> As a general rule, images should not be set to a larger fixed width than 220px (the initial base width), and if an exception to this general rule is warranted, the resulting image should usually be no more than 400px wide (300px for lead images) and 500px tall, for comfortable display on the smallest devices "in common use" (though this may still cause viewing difficulties on some unusual displays).</li></ul>
<p><span id="sizetable"></span>
</p>
<ul><li>To convert a <code>px</code> value to <code><i>scaling factor</i></code>, divide it by 220 and round the result as desired. For example, <code class="nowrap">&#124;150px</code> is roughly equivalent to <code class="nowrap">&#124;upright&#61;0.7</code> (150 / 220 ≃ 0.6818).</li>
<li>To present images larger than the guidelines above (e.g. panoramas), use <code>|thumb|<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:PIC#center" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:PIC">center</a></code> or <code>|thumb|<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:PIC#none" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:PIC">none</a></code>, so that the image stands alone; or use &#123;&#123;<a href="/wiki/Template:Wide_image" title="Template:Wide image">wide image</a>&#125;&#125; or &#123;&#123;<a href="/wiki/Template:Tall_image" title="Template:Tall image">tall image</a>&#125;&#125; to present a very large image in a scrollable box.</li></ul>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Horizontal_placement">Horizontal placement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Images&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Horizontal placement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>Mul&#173;ti&#173;ple im&#173;ages can be stag&#173;gered right and left. How&#173;ever, a&#173;void sand&#173;wich&#173;ing text be&#173;tween two im&#173;ages or cha&#173;rts that face each oth&#173;er; or be&#173;tween an im&#173;age and in&#173;fo&#173;box, nav&#173;i&#173;ga&#173;tion tem&#173;plate, or sim&#173;i&#173;lar. If mul&#173;ti&#173;ple re&#173;lat&#173;ed im&#173;ages are be&#173;ing placed on the rig&#173;ht, con&#173;sid&#173;er the &#123;&#123;<a href="/wiki/Template:Multiple_image" title="Template:Multiple image">multiple image</a>&#125;&#125; tem&#173;plate (which, how&#173;ev&#173;er, does not re&#173;spect <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:IMGSIZE" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:IMGSIZE">logged-in us&#173;ers' se&#173;lect&#173;ed im&#173;age siz&#173;es</a>).
</p><p>In a few web browsers, bulleted lists do not format properly adjacent to left-aligned images; thus it may be preferable to avoid placing a left-aligned image near lists.
</p><p>It is often preferable to place images of people so that they "look" toward the text. (Do not achieve this by reversing the image, which creates a false presentation; faces are never truly symmetric even in the absence of scars or other features.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;nb 4&#93;</a></sup>)
</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Vertical_placement">Vertical placement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Images&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Vertical placement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>An image should generally be placed in the most relevant article section; if this is not possible, try not to place an image "too early" i.e. far ahead of the point in the text discussing what the image illustrates, if this could puzzle the reader.
An image causes a paragraph break (i.e. the current paragraph ends and a new one begins) so it is not possible to place an image <em>within</em> a paragraph. (This applies to <code>thumb</code> images; small inline images are an exception <span role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable selfref">(see Inline images)</span>.)
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="References_from_article_text"><span id="References_from_article"></span>References from article text</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Images&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: References from article text">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r911143475"/><div class="module-shortcutanchordiv"><span id="MOS:SEEIMAGE"></span></div><div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note"><div class="module-shortcutlist"><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a></div><ul><li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=MOS:SEEIMAGE&amp;redirect=no">MOS:SEEIMAGE</a></span></li></ul></div>
<p>Don't refer to images as being to the left/right, above/below, etc. because image placement varies with platform (especially mobile platforms) and screen size, and is meaningless to people using screen readers. Instead, use captions to identify images.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Inline_images">Inline images</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Images&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Inline images">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Help:Visual_file_markup#Border" title="Help:Visual file markup">Help:Visual file markup §&#160;Border</a></div>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r911143475"/><div class="module-shortcutanchordiv"><span id="MOS:BORDERIMAGE"></span></div><div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note"><div class="module-shortcutlist"><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a></div><ul><li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=MOS:BORDERIMAGE&amp;redirect=no">MOS:BORDERIMAGE</a></span></li></ul></div>
<ul><li>Substituting <code>frameless</code> for <code>thumb</code> produces an "inline" image. For example,</li></ul>
<dl><dd><dl><dd><code>This [[File:Flag of Japan.svg|frameless|x20px]] is an inline image.</code></dd></dl></dd>
<dd>produces
<dl><dd>This <a href="/wiki/File:Flag_of_Japan.svg" class="image"><img alt="Flag of Japan.svg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/9e/Flag_of_Japan.svg/30px-Flag_of_Japan.svg.png" decoding="async" width="30" height="20" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/9e/Flag_of_Japan.svg/45px-Flag_of_Japan.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/9e/Flag_of_Japan.svg/60px-Flag_of_Japan.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="900" data-file-height="600" /></a> is an inline image.</dd></dl></dd></dl>
<ul><li>A one-pixel border may be added via <code class="nowrap">&#124;border</code>. For example,</li></ul>
<dl><dd><dl><dd><code>This [[File:Flag of Japan.svg|frameless|x20px|border]] is an inline image with a border.</code></dd></dl></dd>
<dd>produces
<dl><dd>This <a href="/wiki/File:Flag_of_Japan.svg" class="image"><img alt="Flag of Japan.svg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/9e/Flag_of_Japan.svg/30px-Flag_of_Japan.svg.png" decoding="async" width="30" height="20" class="thumbborder" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/9e/Flag_of_Japan.svg/45px-Flag_of_Japan.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/9e/Flag_of_Japan.svg/60px-Flag_of_Japan.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="900" data-file-height="600" /></a> is an inline image with a border.</dd></dl></dd></dl>
<ul><li>Inline images do not have captions</li>
<li>Note the syntax <code>x20px</code>: whereas <code>20px</code> specifies a 20-pixel <em>width</em>, <code>x20px</code> specifies a 20-pixel <em>height</em>. Heights between <code>x18px</code> and <code>x22px</code> will usually match surrounding text well. (<code>upright</code> is not usually used with inline images.)</li></ul>

<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r911143475"/><div class="module-shortcutanchordiv"><span id="MOS:HOTLINK"></span></div><div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note"><div class="module-shortcutlist"><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a></div><ul><li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=MOS:HOTLINK&amp;redirect=no">MOS:HOTLINK</a></span></li></ul></div>
<p><span id="Inline_linking"></span></p><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable"><div style="display:inline;" class="plainlinks selfreference noprint">For further information, see: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Extended_image_syntax" title="Wikipedia:Extended image syntax">Extended image syntax</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Wikimedia_Commons#Embedding_Commons&#39;_media_in_Wikipedia_articles" title="Wikipedia:Wikimedia Commons">Embedding Commons' media in Wikipedia articles</a> as well as <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Uploading_images" title="Wikipedia:Uploading images">Uploading images</a></div></div>
<p>All images used on Wikipedia must be uploaded to Wikipedia itself or <a href="/wiki/Wikimedia_Commons" title="Wikimedia Commons">Wikimedia Commons</a>. Images uploaded to Wikipedia are automatically placed into the <strong><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Namespace#File" title="Wikipedia:Namespace">File namespace</a></strong> (formerly known as the <strong>Image namespace</strong>), i.e., the names of <a href="/wiki/Help:Image_page" class="mw-redirect" title="Help:Image page">image pages</a> start with the prefix <code>File:</code>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Obtaining_images">Obtaining images</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Images&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Obtaining images">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>All images must comply with  <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Image_use_policy" title="Wikipedia:Image use policy">Wikipedia's image use policy</a>: in general, they must be free for reuse, including commercial use and use after alteration, though some "<a href="/wiki/Fair_use" title="Fair use">fair use</a>" of non-free content is allowed in limited circumstances—see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Non-free_content" title="Wikipedia:Non-free content">Wikipedia:Non-free content</a>.
</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Making_images_yourself">Making images yourself <span id="Annotated_image"></span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Images&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Making images yourself">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable"><div style="display:inline;" class="plainlinks selfreference noprint">For further information, see: <a href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Commons:How_to_take_pictures_for_Wikimedia_Commons" class="extiw" title="commons:Commons:How to take pictures for Wikimedia Commons">Commons:How to take pictures</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Graphics_tutorials" title="Wikipedia:Graphics tutorials">Graphics tutorials</a></div></div>
<p>You may upload photographs, drawings, or other graphics created with a camera, scanner, graphics software, and so on. When photographing or scanning potentially copyrighted works, or creating depictions of persons other than yourself, be sure to respect copyright and privacy restrictions.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag needs further explanation.">further explanation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In order to maximize images' usefulness in all languages, avoid including text within them. Instead, add text, links, references, etc., to images using <a href="/wiki/Template:Annotated_image" title="Template:Annotated image">Template:Annotated image</a> or <a href="/wiki/Template:Annotated_image_4" title="Template:Annotated image 4">Template:Annotated image 4</a>, which can also be used to expand the area around an image or crop and enlarge part of an image—all without the need for uploading a new, modified image.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Finding_images_on_the_Internet">Finding images on the Internet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Images&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Finding images on the Internet">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable"><div style="display:inline;" class="plainlinks selfreference noprint">For further information, see: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Finding_images_tutorial" title="Wikipedia:Finding images tutorial">Finding images tutorial</a></div></div>
<p>An extensive list of free image resources by topic can be found at: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Public_domain_image_resources" title="Wikipedia:Public domain image resources">Public domain image resources</a>. In addition to <a href="/wiki/Wikimedia_Commons" title="Wikimedia Commons">Wikimedia Commons</a>, the <a href="https://meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/Toolserver" class="extiw" title="m:Toolserver">Wikimedia Toolserver</a> has a <a href="//toolserver.org/~magnus/fist.php" class="extiw" title="tools:~magnus/fist.php">Free Image Search Tool</a> (FIST), which automatically culls free images from the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Wikimedia_sister_projects" title="Wikipedia:Wikimedia sister projects">Wikimedia sister projects</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flickr" title="Flickr">Flickr</a> and a few other sites. Several other useful, general purpose <a href="/wiki/Image_search" class="mw-redirect" title="Image search">image search</a> engines include: <a href="/wiki/Google_Image_Search" class="mw-redirect" title="Google Image Search">Google Image Search</a>, <a href="/wiki/Picsearch" title="Picsearch">Picsearch</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pixsta" title="Pixsta">Pixsta</a>. <a href="/wiki/Creative_Commons" title="Creative Commons">Creative Commons</a> licensed images with <i>Attribution</i> and <i>Attribution-ShareAlike</i> as their license may be used on Wikipedia. <strong>Images with any license restricting commercial use or the creation of derivative works may not be used on Wikipedia.</strong>
</p><p>The Creative Commons site has a <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://search.creativecommons.org/">search page</a> that can be used as a starting point to find suitably licensed images; make sure you check both the checkboxes "use for commercial purposes" and "modify, adapt, or build upon".
</p><p>If you find an image on the Internet that is not available freely, you can email the copyright owner and ask for their permission to release it under a suitable license, adapting the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Boilerplate_request_for_permission" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Boilerplate request for permission">boilerplate request for permission</a>. If you cannot find a suitable image, you may also list your request at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Requested_pictures" title="Wikipedia:Requested pictures">Wikipedia:Requested pictures</a>, so that another contributor may find or create a suitable image.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Editing_images"><span id="retouching"></span> Editing images</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Images&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Editing images">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Moon_worldwind.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/Moon_worldwind.jpg/220px-Moon_worldwind.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="163" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/Moon_worldwind.jpg/330px-Moon_worldwind.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/Moon_worldwind.jpg/440px-Moon_worldwind.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1280" data-file-height="948" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Moon_worldwind.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>In this <a href="/wiki/Pseudocolor" class="mw-redirect" title="Pseudocolor">pseudocolor</a> image of the moon, red tints represent the highest elevations, purple the lowest; lest the reader be misled, the caption should make clear that this is not the coloring a viewer of the moon would actually see.</div></div></div>
<p>An  image's utility or quality may be improved by cropping (to focus on the relevant portion), cleaning up scanning artifacts, correcting color balance, removing <a href="/wiki/Red-eye_effect" title="Red-eye effect">red-eye effect</a>, or other adjustments.
</p><p>The caption of an image should mention such edits (e.g. introduction of <a href="/wiki/False_color" title="False color">false color or pseudocolor</a>) if a reader needs to know about them to properly interpret the image.
</p><p>Edits that improve the presentation without materially altering the content need not be mentioned in the caption e.g. rotation to correct a slightly crooked image, improvement to the contrast of a scan, or blurring a background to make the main subject more prominent. (However, all changes to images taken from outside sources should be noted on the <a href="#Image_description_pages">image's description page</a>. For images created by editors themselves, changes which could have been part of the image's original composition—such as rotation or minor cropping—need not be mentioned on the description page.)
</p><p>Images should not be changed in ways that materially mislead the viewer. For example, images showing artworks, faces, identifiable places or buildings, or text should not be reversed (although those showing soap bubbles or bacteria might be). Do not change color integral to the subject, such as in images of animals. It is usually appropriate to de-speckle or remove scratches from images, though that might be inappropriate for historical photographs.
</p><p>For assistance in editing images, try <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Graphics_Lab" title="Wikipedia:Graphics Lab">WP:Graphics Lab</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Uploading_images">Uploading images</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Images&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Uploading images">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable"><div style="display:inline;" class="plainlinks selfreference noprint">For further information, see: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Uploading_images" title="Wikipedia:Uploading images">Uploading images</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:File_names" title="Wikipedia:File names">File names</a></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Special:UserLogin" title="Special:UserLogin">Logged in</a> users with <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Autoconfirmed_users" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Autoconfirmed users">autoconfirmed accounts</a> (meaning at least four days old and at least ten edits at the English Wikipedia) can upload media to the English Wikipedia. It is recommended that <strong>only</strong> <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:File_copyright_tags/Free_licenses" title="Wikipedia:File copyright tags/Free licenses">free licensed</a> media, <strong>not</strong> <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Non-free_content" title="Wikipedia:Non-free content">fair use</a> media, be uploaded to <a href="/wiki/Wikimedia_Commons" title="Wikimedia Commons">Wikimedia Commons</a>. Media on Wikimedia Commons can be linked to in the same way as media of the same name on Wikipedia. To upload media to the English Wikipedia, go to <a href="/wiki/Special:Upload" title="Special:Upload">special:upload</a> and for Wikimedia Commons, go to <a href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/special:upload" class="extiw" title="commons:special:upload">commons:special:upload</a>. For preferred file formats, see: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Preparing_images_for_upload" title="Wikipedia:Preparing images for upload">Preparing images for upload</a>.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Image_description_pages">Image description pages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Images&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Image description pages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Categorization#Files/images" title="Wikipedia:Categorization">Wikipedia:Categorization §&#160;Files/images</a></div>
<p>Each image has a corresponding <a href="/wiki/Help:Image_page" class="mw-redirect" title="Help:Image page">description page</a>, which documents the image's source, author and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Copyrights" title="Wikipedia:Copyrights">copyright status</a>; descriptive (who, what, when, where, why) information; and technical (equipment, software, etc.) data useful to readers and later editors.
</p><p>To maximize the utility and educational value of an image, please describe its contents as fully as possible on the image's description page. For example, photographs of artwork benefit from documentation of the artist, title, location, dates, museum identification numbers, and so on. Images that are described only in vague terms (for example, "a cuneiform tablet" or "a medieval manuscript") are often less useful for Wikipedia and less informative to our readers.
</p><p>Reliable sources, if any, may be listed on the image's description page. Generally, Wikipedia assumes in good faith that image creators are correctly identifying the contents of photographs they have taken. If such sources are available, it is helpful to provide them. This is particularly important for technical drawings, as someone may want to verify that the image is accurate.
</p><p>Description pages for images are rediscovered by editors using the <a href="/wiki/H:S" class="mw-redirect" title="H:S">search engine</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Help:Categories" title="Help:Categories">categories</a>. To help editors find precise images, please remember to document the image description page accordingly.  Well-categorized and well-described images are more likely to be used.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Consideration_of_image_download_size">Consideration of image download size</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Images&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Consideration of image download size">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Images can greatly increase the <a href="/wiki/Bandwidth_(computing)" title="Bandwidth (computing)">bandwidth</a> cost of viewing an article&#160;&#8211;&#32;a consideration for readers on slow or expensive connections. Articles carry reduced-size <a href="/wiki/Thumbnail" title="Thumbnail">thumbnails</a> instead of full images (which the user can view by "clicking through" the thumbnail) but in some file types a thumbnail's reduced dimensions doesn't translate into a concomitant reduction in file size. (In most browsers you can see a thumbnail's size by right-clicking for its "Properties".)
</p><p>If one image's file size is disproportionate to those of others in the same article, you may want to reduce it by selecting a different file format:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/GIF" title="GIF">GIF</a> images with a frame size larger than 12.5 million pixels (measured as pixel height × pixel width × number of frames in the animation) cannot currently be displayed in thumbnail form in Wikipedia articles. A thumbnail of a GIF image can be considerably larger in kilobytes than the original image file.</li>
<li>Animated <a href="/wiki/GIF" title="GIF">GIF</a> images have a few additional restrictions. Images larger than 50 million pixels (measured as pixel height × pixel width × number of frames in the animation) currently will only show the first frame of the animation in a thumbnail. When not using a GIF animation at its original frame size, consider creating an Ogg <a href="/wiki/Theora" title="Theora">Theora</a> movie of the animation.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Portable_Network_Graphics" title="Portable Network Graphics">PNG</a> format is useful for storing graphics that contain text, line art, or other images with sharp transitions. It can achieve the same graphical results as a GIF file, and in many cases do so with a higher rate of <a href="/wiki/File_compression" class="mw-redirect" title="File compression">file compression</a>. For this reason, PNG format files are usually preferred to the GIF format.  For images with substantial editing, or for which further editing may be warranted, uploading a PNG as well as a JPEG is common (PNG is lossless compression, so repeatedly saving edits on a PNG will not result in loss of quality.)</li>
<li>A <a href="/wiki/JPEG" title="JPEG">JPEG</a> or other compressed image format can be much smaller than a comparable GIF or PNG format file. When there is no apparent difference in quality, such as with a photograph that has no sharp graphical transitions, a compressed image format such as JPEG may be preferable for reasons of download performance. Wikipedia is often able to achieve much better compression of JPEG photograph thumbnails than comparable PNG images, and with little perceptible loss of quality. Repeatedly loading and resaving an image as JPEG will result in loss of quality, however, as will using low settings for the JPEG; as such, if you've made edits, it can be helpful to save a PNG or TIFF copy before closing the image editor and upload that as well; this copy can then be used to generate a new JPEG after further editing.</li>
<li>Where an image consists solely of line art, charts text and simple graphics, an <a href="/wiki/Scalable_Vector_Graphics" title="Scalable Vector Graphics">SVG</a> file can be significantly smaller than other graphics formats. This is because the data is encoded as a series of drawing commands, rather than as <a href="/wiki/Raster_graphics" title="Raster graphics">raster graphics</a>. There are <a href="/wiki/Open_source" title="Open source">open source</a> applications available for rendering graphics in SVG format. However, SVG thumbnails are rendered as PNGs.</li>
<li>Rather than including an image gallery on an article, which could add significantly to the download size, consider creating a gallery/category on the Wikimedia Commons instead.</li></ul>

<li id="cite_note-typical-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-typical_1-0">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">

<li id="cite_note-leave_pref-2"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-leave_pref_2-0">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">

<li id="cite_note-corresp-3"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-corresp_3-0">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">
<code>px</code> works the same as <code>upright</code> for users with the usual base width setting of 220px, but works counterintuitively for readers whose base width is set to a different value (see <a href="/wiki/Help:Preferences#Files" title="Help:Preferences">Help:Preferences#Files</a>). For example, an image coded <code>275px</code>—presumably to make it wider than most images on a particular page—is actually rendered <em>smaller</em> than most images if the user has changed his base width to 300px. In contrast, <code>upright</code> responds gracefully to changes in the user's base width, maintaining the relative size of images in any given article by enlarging or reducing all of them proportionately.
<div class="paragraphbreak" style="margin-top:0.5em"></div>
<p>However, a thumbnail can <strong>not</strong> be displayed larger than the original uploaded image. For example, if an image is coded <code>|thumb|330px</code> or <code>|thumb|upright=1.5</code> (for a reader with the usual base width of 220px), but the original uploaded file was only 200px wide, then the article thumbnail will still be displayed at only 200px.
</p>
